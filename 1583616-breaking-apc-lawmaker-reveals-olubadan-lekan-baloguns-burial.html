BREAKING: APC Lawmaker, Akin Alabi, Speaks on Next Olubadan

  • An APC House of Representative member from Oyo state, Oloye Akin Alabi, has disclosed that the next Olubadan is not former governor Rashidi Ladoja
  • Alabi made the comments in his reaction to the controversy on who will be the next Olubadan, as shared by former presidential aide Tolu Ogunlesi
  • According to the lawmaker, the next Olubadan would be High Chief Owolabi Olakukehin, who is the current Balogun of Ibadanland

Ibadan, Oyo - Oloye Akin Alabi, an All Progressives Congress (APC) lawmaker representing Egbeda/Ona-Ara federal constituency in Oyo state, has disclosed that the successor of the late Oba Lekan Balogun, the Olubadan of Ibadan, was not former governor Rashidi Ladoja as claimed.

In a tweet, the lawmaker responded to the claim by former presidential aide Tolu Ogunlesi, who has tipped the former governor as the next in line to become the Olubadan because of the existing tradition in one of the largest city.

According to Alabi, the next Olubadan will be High Chief Owolabi Olakulehin. Olakukehin is next in line with the existing tradition.

Governor Makinde announces Olubadan's death

Governor Seyi Makinde of Oyo state announced the demise of Oba Balogun on Thursday evening, March 14, in a statement shortly after his departure at the University College Hospital (UCH) Ibadan following a short illness.

Soon after his death, there were controversies about who would become the next Olubadan because ascension to the throne was promotional, unlike in other traditions of the Yoruba.
