George Washington Bridge: How Much Does It Cost to Not Swim Across?

Let's face it, tolls can be a real drag. You're cruising along, enjoying the scenery, singing terribly to the radio (we've all been there), and then BAM! A tollbooth appears, ready to gobble up your hard-earned cash. But fear not, fellow traveler, for today we delve into the delightful world of George Washington Bridge tolls!

The Great Tollsby: E-ZPass vs. Pay-by-Pain

There are two main ways to pay your George Washington Bridge toll:

  • E-ZPass: This little electronic buddy whizzes you right through the toll lane with barely a blip. Think of it as the Invisibility Cloak for tolls (minus the whole invisibility part). It's also the cheapest option! During peak hours (when everyone and their uncle seems to be on the bridge), an E-ZPass will set you back $15.38. Off-peak travelers score a steal at $13.38.
  • Pay-by-Mail or Cash (RIP): For those without E-ZPass, there's the scenic route – mailing in a toll bill or using the tollbooth (though be warned, these haven't seen cash since flip phones were cool). This option comes with a hefty $17.63 price tag, so you might want to consider investing in some carrier pigeons to save some dough (don't do that, it's illegal... and messy).

Remember: Westbound travel (New Jersey to New York) is toll-free, so you can pretend you're a pioneer forging a new westward path (minus the dysentery, hopefully).

Peak Hours: When Frugality Meets Frustration

Peak hours on the George Washington Bridge are like a disco party – everyone's there, and it can get a little crowded (and sweaty, if you're stuck in traffic). These glorious times are:

  • Weekdays: 6 AM to 10 AM and 4 PM to 8 PM
  • Weekends: 11 AM to 9 PM

If you're a penny pincher (and who isn't these days?), avoid these times to snag the cheaper off-peak toll rate.

FAQ: Your George Washington Bridge Toll Troubles Solved (Hopefully)

  • How do I get an E-ZPass? Head over to [E-ZPass website] (https://www.e-zpassny.com/) to set up your account and say goodbye to tollbooth woes!
  • What if I forget my E-ZPass? No worries! You'll get a bill in the mail, but be prepared to pay the $17.63 toll rate.
  • Can I use any E-ZPass on the George Washington Bridge? Yep! Most E-ZPass systems from other states are compatible.
  • Is there a way to avoid tolls altogether? Sure, if you have a jetpack or a strong swimming stroke. Otherwise, you're out of luck (unless you go westbound, see above).
  • Do motorcycles pay tolls? They do! The rate depends on the type of motorcycle, but it's generally cheaper than cars.

So there you have it! Hopefully, this lightens the load (pun intended) of figuring out George Washington Bridge tolls. Now get out there and explore, just remember to budget for the toll unless you're planning on swimming alongside the fish!
