The Statler Brothers (sometimes known as simply The Statlers in country music circles) were an American country music, gospel, and backup vocal group for Johnny Cash that performed in the 1960s and 1970s. The quartet was established in the Virginia town of Staunton in 195Originally known as The Four Star Quartet, the group went on to play gospel music at various churches around the area under the name The Kingsmen. When the song “Louie, Louie” by the garage rock band The Kingsmen, also known as The Kingsmen, became popular in 1963, the band decided to rebrand themselves as The Statler Brothers to capitalise on their fame. The group’s name belies the fact that just two of its members (Don and Harold Reid) are biological brothers, and none of them have the surname Statler. 

In reality, the band took its name from a particular brand of face tissue that they had discovered in a hotel room (they joked that they could have turned out to be the Kleenex Brothers). Due to poor health, Lew DeWitt was replaced by Jimmy Fortune as the main singer and guitarist of the Statlers in 198Don Reid was the lead singer, Harold Reid was the bass singer, Phil Balsley sang baritone, and Don Reid sung tenor and was the guitarist of the Statlers. Sadly, DeWitt passed away on August 15, 1990, as a result of heart and renal problems caused by complications of Crohn’s disease.  The gospel origins of the band were evident in the style of the group. “We took church harmonies and placed them over in country music,” explains Harold Reid. The group maintained a strong connection to its gospel origins, with a majority of their albums including at least one gospel song as a part of their repertoire. They recorded a tribute song to the Blackwood Brothers, who had an impact on their music, and released numerous gospel-only CDs as a result of their efforts. The Statler Brothers also composed a song as a homage to Johnny Cash, who was the one who introduced them to each other. The song was titled “We Got Paid by Cash,” and it was written to commemorate their time spent with Cash.
